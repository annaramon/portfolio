About me

I grew up in a village in the Catalan Pyrenees. I learned to read mountain conditions before I learned to code and that instinct for finding signal in noise is exactly what I do now.

Today I build injury-risk prediction systems for elite football clubs, including FC Barcelona — using wearable, GPS, biomarker, and genetics data to protect athletes. Before that, I built clinical AI for neonatal ICUs that reduced dosage errors from 12.49% to 0.03%.

I also teach what I build, from sports analytics at the Barça Innovation Hub to ML engineering at UPC. Bridging the lab and the real world is how I stay sharp.

The problems I care about sit where AI meets the human body, where getting it right isn't optional.

How I Work

Not what tools I use — how I think.

End-to-End Ownership

From raw data to production. I don't hand off — I build the pipeline, train the model, design the evaluation, deploy it, and iterate. The whole thing.

Built for the User, Not the Metric

My models are used by doctors and coaches — people who don't read confusion matrices. I design interpretable outputs and evaluation frameworks that non-technical staff can trust and act on.

Feedback Loops Over One-Shot Models

I build systems that learn from real user interactions — not models that ship and stagnate. Continuous evaluation, real-world feedback, constant iteration.

Signal in Noise

Wearables, GPS, blood biomarkers, genetics — the data I work with is messy, high-frequency, and multimodal. I build pipelines that extract what matters from real-world chaos.

Beyond the Code

What drives me outside the terminal.

Sport as a Lifestyle

For me, sport has never been a hobby — it's how I cope with the day to day, how I reset, how I push through. I run, bike, swim, and hike. I've completed several half marathons. I push my limits because that's where growth lives. Sport teaches me resilience, discipline, and consistency — the same principles that drive my engineering work.

My Own Data Subject

I've worn a wearable for years — tracking heart rate, sleep, training load, recovery. I don't just build AI for athletes; I self-analyze my own metrics to adapt my training, test hypotheses on myself, and understand from the inside what it means to turn biometric data into better decisions. That lived experience makes me a sharper engineer.

Teaching & Mentoring

I've mentored 100+ students and teach across postgraduate programs. Explaining complex ideas simply is a superpower — it makes me a better engineer, communicator, and teammate.

What Excites Me

I believe the next wave of AI impact won't come from bigger models — it will come from systems that understand the human body. Wearables, biomarkers, genetics, and real-time physiological data are generating signals we've barely begun to decode.

I want to build the AI that turns those signals into earlier interventions, smarter prevention, and better human performance — whether that's on the pitch, in the ICU, or in everyday life. The intersection of AI, health, and human data is where I want to make my biggest mark.
